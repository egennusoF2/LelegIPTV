## Sign in

The login screen has one **Server URL** field plus optional **Username** / **Password**. How you fill it in depends on what your provider gave you.

### Xtream Codes

If your provider sent you a host, a port, a username, and a password, fill in:

- **Server URL**: your provider's host, plus the port if they gave you one. For example `example-iptv.tv:8080` or `http://example-iptv.tv`
- **Username** and **Password**: exactly as your provider sent them.

<Callout type="tip">
  Many providers also send a long "player_api.php" URL with the username and password baked in. Paste that whole URL into **Server URL** and the app auto-fills the username and password fields for you - again, the `http://` prefix is optional.
</Callout>

### M3U / M3U8 URL

If your provider only gave you a single playlist URL (anything ending in `.m3u` or `.m3u8`, or a `get.php?...` link), paste the **whole URL** into **Server URL** and leave Username and Password blank. The app detects the M3U format and switches modes.

## Multiple playlists

You can add as many Xtream accounts and M3U URLs as you want. Open **Settings > Playlists** to add, rename, or remove them. Switch the active one from the sidebar.

Favorites, watchlist, recents, and watch progress are tracked **per playlist** so different accounts stay separate. Favorites, Watchlist, and Continue watching also offer a combined view across all your playlists.

## Hide unwanted categories

Many providers ship hundreds of categories you don't want to see ("Adult", regional channels, dead links). Open **Settings > Categories** (or the filter button on Live TV / Movies / Series) to toggle which ones show up. Hidden categories stay hidden across restarts and are remembered per playlist.

## EPG looks shifted

If the EPG (now / next, full schedule) looks off by a few hours, your provider is sending timestamps in their own timezone rather than UTC. Open **Settings > Live TV** and adjust the **EPG offset** for the active playlist. The full schedule is on the **EPG** page in the sidebar.

## Backup and restore

**Settings > Backup** exports a single JSON file containing your playlists, preferences, and app settings. Import it on another device to bring everything across.

<Callout type="warning">
  The export includes your credentials in plain JSON. Treat the file like a password manager export: don't email it, don't drop it in a shared folder, and delete it from any temporary location once you've restored on the target device.
</Callout>
